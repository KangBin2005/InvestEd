What is Compound Interest?

🤔 In plain English: Your money makes money. Then THAT money makes money. It's like a snowball rolling downhill - it gets bigger and bigger.
📖 Let me show you:

Imagine you put $100 in an account that gives you 10% every year.

  • Year 1: You earn $10 → Total = $110
  • Year 2: You earn $11 (10% of $110) → Total = $121
  • Year 3: You earn $12 → Total = $133

💡 See what happened? You earned MORE in Year 2 ($11) than Year 1 ($10) even though you didn't add any money. That's compound interest!

Why Rate of Return Matters

🎯 The short version: Rate of return = how fast your money grows. Higher rate = faster growth. But higher rate usually means more risk.
💰 Real example:

Same $1,000 invested for 20 years:

  • 🏦 Savings Account (1%) → grows to $1,220
  • 📜 Bonds (4%) → grows to $2,191
  • 📊 Stocks (8%) → grows to $4,661

💡 That's a $3,441 difference between savings and stocks - from the same $1,000!

Risk vs Reward

⚠️ The trade-off: Want higher returns? You have to accept that your money might go DOWN some years. It's like a roller coaster - higher highs, but scarier drops.
📊 Real history (1926-2022):
  • 🟢 Savings accounts: Never lost money. But only grew ~3% per year.
  • 🔴 Stocks: Averaged ~10% per year. BUT had 14 years where they lost money (sometimes -30% or more!).

💡 The key question: When do you need the money? If you need it in 2 years → pick low risk. If you have 20+ years → higher risk can pay off.

What is Financial Freedom?

🏆 The goal: When your investments earn enough money each year to cover your living expenses. You don't NEED to work anymore.
🎯 Simple formula:

Freedom Number = Yearly Spending ÷ Expected Return

Example: You spend $50,000/year and expect 5% returns:

$50,000 ÷ 0.05 = $1,000,000

You need $1 million to never work again!
